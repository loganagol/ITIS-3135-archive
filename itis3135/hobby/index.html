Welcome

Welcome to The Supplemental Iron. This site is about my weight training journey throughout the years. It is intended to serve as a resource for my current progress and gym programming.

I really enjoy weightlifting, although it took me a while to really get into it. It gives me self-confidence and focus, keeps me healthy, and helps me sleep. I recommend it to anyone who's anxious or has trouble sleeping.

About

Getting Started

I first started resistance training in July 2016, starting off with the Recommended Routine from Reddit's r/bodyweightfitness. I stuck with that off and on for a long time, but I struggled with discipline and progression after about a year. I had pretty much stopped exercising regularly, and would only do bodyweight exercises maybe once a week. I started weightlifting in March 2019 after getting frustrated with my boredom and lack of progress in bodyweight exercises. I was enjoying myself and progressing well until the COVID-19 pandemic hit in early 2020 and closed most gyms. After several months of searching through price gouged items on Facebook Marketplace I found some reasonable sellers and put together a basic garage gym, but lacked determination to work out regularly.

I didn't get serious or disciplined about gym time until August 2021, when I went back to school to pursue my bachelor's degree and needed an outlet and hobby to work off the stress. Judas Priest really helped me during that time, they kept me going and my spirits up. Music has continued to be a big factor in keeping me motivated and focused.

Motivation

My main motivation for working out is to reduce anxiety and help me sleep. I have chronic issues with falling asleep and a combination of weight training and cardio nearly fixes them. As long as I stay disciplined I can sleep soundly at night. I'm also kind of a nerdy, skinny guy and I enjoy the self confidence that comes from bulking up.

Routine Programming

This is my current routine. It follows a basic PPL (Pull Push Legs) split across multiple days of the week. This means that one day is devoted to exercises where I push weight away from myself, one day is for exercises where I pull weight towards myself, and one day is for leg and core exercises.

It's a lot of work, and I'm thinking of adding in a second rest day. When I first started out I ran a "bro split" with more minimal daily workouts, which has influenced my current schedule.
