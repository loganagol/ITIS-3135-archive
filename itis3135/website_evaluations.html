Website Evaluations


Mandiant Security Blog

This website is the public blog for Mandiant, a cybersecurity company. The blog contains updates on company products, efforts, security threats, and incident response writeups.

  • Contrast:
    The contrast of the website is good, relying on darkness and brightness with minimal color. The small amounts of color that are used are bright red, used for emphasis. The color choice pops against both white and black backgrounds equally well. Contrast is also shown in the text sizes, which clearly delineate titles and content.
  • Repetition:
    The repetition is also good. There are a large number of blog posts on each page, but each one has a uniform format (color, size, and content). There are a lot of posts on the screen but regular formatting and use of a limited color palette prevent it becoming overwhelming.
  • Alignment:
    Alignment is used here to separate the other page content from the blogposts. It's also used to show the difference between the title and information of each blog post.
  • Proximity:
    Proximity is extremely important when there are many different items in one place, and the designers here realize that. Each post is separated from another by both margin and spacing between titles. The main page content is also separated from the blog posts by lack of proximity. Whereas blog post information is kept very close to the titles.
Hacker News

Hacker News is a user-submitted content aggregation site similar to Reddit. It predominately caters to more technologically-inclined users. Users submit links to interesting sites, upvote others posts, and comment with deeply nested replies.

  • Contrast:
    The only real colors used are black, white, and orange, but the orange is only used to emphasize the title bar at the top. There is also very little size contrast between different elements. UI elements and content are largely identical in size and color.
  • Repetition:
    There are 30 posts per page, and in this case the repetition makes it hard to read. Every element looks like a repeat of the last, and the large numbers of similar posts blur together.
  • Alignment:
    The alignment isn't terrible, but it could be better. Post information is jumbled together at the left under each entry, and the top header bar is aligned with the posts. However, at least the footer is center aligned, which helps it stand out from the page content.
  • Proximity:
    The page content is far too close together. The post points, title, information, and link are also too close together. There are small margins on the left and the right, but the nav bard, header, footer, and content could really use larger margins or padding. Especially between the posts.
