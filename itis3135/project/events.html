Dock Diving/Jumping

In dock diving, dogs compete for distance by jumping off a dock into a body of water. Docks are typically ~40 feet long and ~2 feet above the surface of the water.

Divisions include:

  • Novice
  • Junior
  • Senior
  • Master/Pro
  • Ultimate/Extreme/Elite
  • Super Elite
Obedience Trial

In an obedience trial, dogs compete for points by responding to their handler's commands. Common commands are "sit", "stay", "heel", and "come". Every passing score in a trial contributes towards an obedience title.

Another type of task a dog may perform is scent discrimination. A handler will place their scent on a leather, metal, or wooden object and place it on the ground at a distance. The dog will then retrieve the scented object mixed with its identical counterparts.

Titles include (with some variation):

  • CD (Companion Dog)
  • CDX (Companion Dog Excellent)
  • UD (Utility Dog)
  • UDX (Utility Dog Excellent)
  • OTCh (Ability Trial Champion)
Hunt Test

In a Hunt Test, dogs compete to pass the requirements of the test. Requirements include but are not limited to:

  • Finding a hidden bird within a field.
  • Pointing the location of a hidden bird.
  • Remaining steady on point while the handlers and judges move into position.
  • Not chasing a "flushed" bird.
  • Marking the fall location and retrieving the bird once it has been shot.
  • "Honoring" another dog's point if the another dog finds the bird first.
  • Working with the handler and listening to their directions in the field.

Hunt test levels include:

  • Junior
  • Senior
  • Master
